Hidden Trails of Haa Valley – 7 Days

Venture off the beaten path into the tranquil Haa Valley — one of Bhutan’s least explored yet most enchanting destinations. Tucked between mountain ridges, this serene valley offers sweeping alpine views, quiet hamlets, and age-old monasteries untouched by mass tourism. Traverse scenic trails, cross traditional wooden bridges, and experience Bhutan’s rural life where prayer flags flutter above yak pastures and ancient legends whisper through pine forests.

Tour Itinerary keyboard_arrow_down

Day 1 – Arrival in Paro keyboard_arrow_down

Land at Paro International Airport and meet your guide. Visit the National Museum and Paro Dzong, then enjoy an evening stroll through the charming streets of Paro. Overnight in Paro.

Day 2 – Paro to Haa Valley via Chelela Pass keyboard_arrow_down

Drive over the spectacular Chelela Pass (3,988 m) — one of Bhutan’s highest motorable passes — offering panoramic views of the Himalayas and Mount Jomolhari. Descend into the pristine Haa Valley and visit Lhakhang Karpo and Lhakhang Nagpo, the legendary White and Black Temples. Overnight in Haa.

Day 3 – Village Walks and Farm Experience in Haa keyboard_arrow_down

Spend the day exploring rural Bhutanese life. Walk through small villages, meet local families, and learn about traditional agriculture. Enjoy a home-cooked Bhutanese lunch at a farmhouse and an evening cultural performance by locals. Overnight in Haa.

Day 4 – Hike to Juneydrak Hermitage keyboard_arrow_down

Take a scenic hike through pine forests to the Juneydrak Hermitage, a sacred site perched on a cliff that houses a footprint of Guru Rinpoche. Along the trail, enjoy stunning views of Haa’s emerald valley below. Return for an evening bonfire. Overnight in Haa.

Day 5 – Haa to Paro keyboard_arrow_down

Drive back to Paro, stopping en route for photo opportunities along Chelela Pass. In Paro, visit Kyichu Lhakhang — one of Bhutan’s oldest and most sacred temples. Evening at leisure. Overnight in Paro.

Day 6 – Hike to Taktsang Monastery (Optional) keyboard_arrow_down

For those seeking adventure, hike to the iconic Tiger’s Nest (Taktsang Monastery). Alternatively, enjoy a relaxed day exploring Paro town and its markets. Evening cultural dinner. Overnight in Paro.

Day 7 – Departure keyboard_arrow_down

After breakfast, transfer to Paro International Airport for your departure, leaving with memories of Bhutan’s untouched valleys and warm mountain hospitality.

Highlights keyboard_arrow_down

Traverse one of Bhutan’s most scenic routes over Chelela Pass, uncover the legends of Haa Valley’s twin temples, and walk through timeless Himalayan villages. Perfect for travellers seeking quiet, authenticity, and gentle adventure amidst pristine alpine landscapes.

Travel Information & Essentials keyboard_arrow_down

The Haa Valley is best visited between March–May and September–November, when skies are clear and mountain views are most vivid. Haa sits at around 2,700 m elevation, so light jackets are useful in the evenings. Respect local customs — especially at sacred sites — and always ask before photographing people in rural areas.
