Everything You Need to Know Before You Go

Q1: Do I need a visa to travel to Bhutan?

keyboard_arrow_down

Yes, most international visitors require a visa to travel to Bhutan. Your visa will be arranged through an authorized Bhutanese tour operator — like us — as part of your travel package. Simply share a copy of your passport, and we’ll handle the entire application process for you. (Note: Indian, Bangladeshi, and Maldivian nationals can obtain entry permits on arrival.)

Q2: What’s the best time of year to visit Bhutan?

keyboard_arrow_down

The best times to visit Bhutan are spring (March–May) and autumn (September–November) when the weather is clear, skies are bright, and festivals such as Paro Tshechu and Thimphu Tshechu take place. If you prefer fewer crowds and lush green landscapes, the summer and winter months offer quieter, equally rewarding experiences.

Q3: Are flights to Bhutan included in the tour package?

keyboard_arrow_down

Flights to Bhutan can be arranged upon request. We can book your tickets with Drukair or Bhutan Airlines, the two national carriers operating flights into Paro International Airport. If you prefer, you can also manage your own flights — our packages are flexible.

Q4: Is Bhutan suitable for solo travelers?

keyboard_arrow_down

Absolutely! Bhutan is one of the safest and most welcoming destinations for solo travelers. You’ll have a dedicated guide and driver throughout your trip, ensuring comfort, safety, and a personalized experience. Many of our guests travel solo to immerse themselves in Bhutan’s peaceful culture and stunning Himalayan landscapes.

Q5: Can you customize the itinerary for my preferences?

keyboard_arrow_down

Yes, all of our itineraries are fully customizable. Whether you’re seeking cultural immersion, trekking adventures, photography tours, or wellness retreats, we’ll tailor your trip to match your interests, schedule, and pace. Our goal is to create a truly personalized Bhutan experience for every traveler.
